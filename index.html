Job Market Paper

Job Market Paper
Simple Strategies in Information Ripples
PDF Slides
Abstract

This paper introduces a framework for decentralised games with rank-dependent payoffs, built on a payoff-derived anchor, a distance-decaying perception kernel, and one round of best response. Its flagship result: in environments where Nash equilibrium is *maximally* indeterminate — when type distributions have independent marginals, any allocation of agents across actions is self-fulfilling — my framework is *maximally* sharp, producing a unique prediction for every type. Projection models of auctions \[Breitmoser 2019; Gagnon-Bartsch–Pagnozzi–Rosato 2021\] and level-kk k bidding \[Crawford–Iriberri 2007\] emerge as special kernels. Applications: first-price auctions, subject choice under Chinese Gaokao reforms, and occupational sorting.

Working Papers

Learning-Optimal Design of Data Markets
with Yangbo Song
[Revise & Resubmit, Journal Name] · [Year]
PDF
Abstract

We study the design of data markets with strategic role selection. A continuum of agents each receive an i.i.d. private signal regarding an uncertain state, and decides whether to become a seller who trades off their signal for monetary revenue, a buyer who receives a signal from sellers and subsequently acts based on two signals, or a non-participant who acts solely according to their own signal. Each agent is better off when they make a costly final investment matching the true state. A market designer commits to a transfer function and aims to optimize information aggregation among agents. For state-independent markets where transfers only rely on an agent’s role and the overall measures of buyers and sellers, e.g. the standardized data trading platforms, we show the robust existence of a “digital market for lemons” even under the optimal design: the least informed agents sell, the moderately informed buy, while the most informed opt out. Nevertheless, a statedependent market which links the state to sellers’ revenue, e.g. blockchain-empowered networks offering data-quality-based rewards, may reverse the role distribution and restore information efficiency. Under the optimal design, the most informed agent(s) sell their signal to all others, whose aggregate action achieves maximum possible correctness in markets with a single transaction. Moreover, while the designer must pay a net subsidy for implementing a learning-optimal state-independent market, she can make a positive profit from a state-dependent one.
